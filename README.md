# Global-surface-urban-heat-island--SUHI--intensity-and-footprint-dataset--2005-2024-
The global surface urban heat island (SUHI) footprint has expanded dramatically over the past two decades, redefining our understanding of urban warming.

This repository contains the core code, analytical pipelines, and visualization procedures supporting the research presented in the paper titled “A new global surface urban heat island dataset reveals significant footprint expansion but stable intensity.” The project focuses on applying an iterative thermal algorithm (ITA) using radial logistic fitting to explicitly resolve the spatial decay of land surface temperature, enabling the simultaneous delineation of the SUHI footprint (SUHIF) and quantification of SUHI intensity (SUHII) across >30,000 cities globally.

The key functionalities include:
⦁	City-level and latitudinal statistical profiling of the global SUHI dataset, focusing on both SUHII and its temporal trends.
⦁	Non-parametric trend analysis, executing Mann-Kendall (MK) tests and Sen's slope estimator to evaluate long-term (2005–2024) variations in global surface UHI intensity.
⦁	Climate zone-stratified statistics quantifying global SUHII, SUHII trends, SUHIF expansion areas, and overall global SUHI extent dynamics.
⦁	Pixel-level regression validation comparing the proposed ITA-derived SUHI dataset against results generated by the Simplified Urban-Extent (SUE) algorithm and Distance-Decay (DEA) method, scaled globally and by climate zones.
⦁	Comparative evaluation of the SUHI datasets derived from the three methods (ITA, SUE, and DEA) utilizing Cumulative Distribution Functions (CDFs).
⦁	Quantitative and comparative morphological analysis of the urban cores and SUHIF utilizing the Fractal Dimension Index (FRAC) for individual cities globally.
⦁	Spatial proximity analysis measuring the geometric distances between the SUHIF boundaries and urban core areas across global and regional climate extents.
⦁	Correlation analysis investigating the interactions and driving mechanisms between global SUHIF expansion and physical urban land expansion.
⦁	Compilation of comprehensive summary tables (.xlsx) encompassing the complete global SUHI dataset derived from Terra and Aqua satellite Land Surface Temperature (LST) records.
⦁	Programmatic generation and rendering of publication-quality statistical figures in SVG format.

There are four primary folders under this repository.
Codes_for_analysis: Houses the core analytical pipelines and statistical engines. This includes scripts for long-term trend analysis (MK test and Sen's slope), spatial distance calculations, morphological quantification (FRAC), and the primary data processing workflows required to construct the SUHII and SUHIF datasets.
Codes_for_svg: Contains the rendering scripts designed to visualize spatial morphologies, spatial resolutions of the UHI effect, and the consistency validation between the ITA-generated dataset and the DEA/SUE baseline algorithms.
Svg: Stores the final output visual representations (publication-quality SVG files) generated by the scripts in the Codes_for_svg directory.
Table: Contains comprehensive summary datasets and annual fitting performance metrics for the Terra and Aqua-derived SUHI datasets. This includes detailed statistical results for SUHII, SUHIF, FRAC, and UHI morphological characteristics.

Detailed usage instructions, function dependencies, and parameter explanations are documented within the header of each script.
